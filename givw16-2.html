Microsoft Garage 2016 Internship

Project Management

Central Design Team @ Microsoft Garage

(Image from Microsoft)

THE TEAM:
Wendy Lu, Nathan Lam, Juan Henao, Sara Kiani

THE CHALLENGE:
The challenge presented to the central design team at the Garage intern group was for us 5 designers to work on 4 projects simultaneously. The design team struggled with this initially, and reformed the the structure of the team multiple times. It was finally decided to formalize the team to having one project manager, to which I was given the role.

MY CONTRIBUTIONS AS DESIGN TEAM PROJECT MANAGER:

  • Helped smooth communication between design team, developers, and sponsors
  • Relayed information, concerns, and questions to the design team
  • Helped focus direction of projects if needed
  • Moved design resources between projects if needed


CONTEXT:
The Vancouver Garage internship program facilitates the collaboration of groups of students on projects with the goal of shipping them as Microsoft products at the end of 4 months. This term, there were 19 developers and 5 designers collaborating on 4 projects.

Previously, teams at the Garage had one designer per project, with no collaboration between them. However, designers work best when collaborating and bouncing ideas off of each other. This time, Stephane (Garage Program Manager) decided to establish a central team of five designers that would work together on all projects simultaneously.

This design model proved to be more difficult than imagined, and was improved upon several times throughout the internship. By the end, each designer worked on two projects, with one central PM amongst them. The skills we learned from this experience have been invaluable.

MODEL 1: 1 DESIGNER, 2 PROJECTS. 2 WEEK ROTATIONS.
Sprint 0-1

  • Poor communication with developers
  • Too much time spent in every projects' meetings
  • Too much time spent syncing up within Design Team for each project
  • No time to do actual design work

MODEL 2: 1 DESIGNER, 1 PROJECTS. 1 WEEK ROTATIONS.
Sprint 2-6

  • Complete hand-off to another designer each week
  • 2 days wasted each week to catch up to speed
  • Regression in design work

MODEL 3: 1 DESIGNER, 2 PROJECTS. NO ROTATIONS, but 1 PM.
Sprint 6-END
My Controbutions as PM of Design Team:

  • Helped smooth communication between design team, developers, and sponsors
  • Relayed information, concerns, and questions to the design team
  • Helped focus direction of projects if needed
  • Moved design resources between projects if needed
