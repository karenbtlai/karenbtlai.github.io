REDESIGN OF FABRIC STEAMERS

RESEARCH TEAM:
Athena Yuen, Abdullah Usman, Maryum Mussa, Chi-Lan Nguyen

PROBLEM STATEMENT
Steamers are a growing market as they start to replace traditional irons and other fabric de-wrinkling products. As population density continues to grow and living space continues to diminish at urban and suburban neighbourhoods, there exists a need for more space efficient fabric care products. Thus, steamers, or more specifically hand-held steamers, have been booming in popularity in the recent years. However, steamers have not improved and adapted to this sudden demand: there has been no significant change or progress in its design since it was first introduced. This leaves us an excellent opportunity to re-design and improve the existing product.

The preliminary research of hand-held fabric steamers is done as a team of 5. This includes investigations in user values and target market, context and user cycle, safety, maintenance, ergonomics, materials, mechanisms, future technologies and oppotunities.

Based off the presented context and opportunities, the final design of the fabric steamer is individual.
