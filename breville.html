BREVILLE SMART GRINDER

Breville Smart Grinder

HUMAN-USE EXPERIENCE ANALYSIS

THE TEAM:
Josephine Luu, Thomas Wagner, Gabriella Hong, Caitlin McCulloh, Mia Muscroft, Chi-Lan Nguyen, Samantha Astles

CONTEXT:
Breville is a company known to re-innovate existing technologies by digitizing analog components. One example is their series of expensive and seemingly 'high-tech' home kitchen appliances that targets the modern, tech-savvy upper-middle class or above. These appliances' design intent is primarily style and ease of use. As Breville tends to digitize analog components for their appliances, they have the freedom to re-imagine and improve the user-cycle of their appliances for a more pleasurable user-experience. However, this dissonance from traditional kitchen appliances may not always be advantageous, and create a slight learning curve for some users.

Our team analyzed and presented the Breville Smart Grinder's product life cycle, user cycle, anthropometrics, ergonomics, physical and digital user interface, user experience, as well as safety and manufacturing.

MY CONTRIBUTIONS:
I analyzed the user interface aspect of the Breville Smart Grinder. This includes the digital graphical user interface and the physical interface that accompanies and interacts with the digital display.

CONCLUSION:
While this product's sleek, "high-tech" look does strike well with most, some trade-offs are made for the appearance. This product stays true to Breville's brand identity, and for this reason does market quite well towards their target market. However, some trade-offs take away from vital functionalities, and that invokes the question whether this appliance's digitization of analog components is really worth the extra cost.
