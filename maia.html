Maia: Wearable Technology for Contraception or Planned Parenthood

Maia

Personal Project for School

(Original icons from FreePik)

The PDF version of the project can be found here:

  • .PDF File

CONTEXT:
The premise of the school project is to create a product that follows the critical project drivers: a set driver created at the beginning of the project outlining product goals and restrictions. The emerging product is a wearable bracelet that takes one wrist temperature measurement per day. This is the basal temperature, and is used to calculate a woman's ovulation cycle. Only 5/28 days is a woman fertile: the chances of conceiving outside of those 5 days fall to 0%. Thus, the bracelet signifies the user when to use additional protection during intercourse. It provides new method for contraception and planned parenthood. It is non-intrusive, non-hormonal, and has no side-effects

It offers a much better user experience than the only competition. Furthermore, the aesthetics of the wearable does not deter women from using it, as it blends in with other jewelry items. It uses an electhrochromic film to signify the user during ovulation days, rather than an LED that announces to the rest of the world what ovulation day she is on. Its housing can also be redesigned for a lower cost to be used in developing countries.
